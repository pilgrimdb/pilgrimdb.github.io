The coastal route from Porto, the Camino Portugués de la Costa, also known as the Caminho do Noroeste or Caminho do Atlântico. For the Portuguese section, see caminador.es. See also Camara Municipal for brochure on the section through Vila do Conde; Amigos in Viana do Castelo; Associação Jacobeia dos Caminhos de Esposende.
There are spurs to and from the main Barcelos route at São Pedro de Rates, and also a link along the Minho to Valença.
On the Galician side, a route is marked from A Guarda via the monastery at Oia to Vigo, joining the Central route at Redondela. This route is also known as the Camino Monacal and according to a local tradition was the pilgrimage route used by Thomas Becket. A guide is produced by the Asociación Amigos de los Pazos, and see A Guarda concello.
This route has been officially adopted by the Xunta, but to date no work has been done on it.
