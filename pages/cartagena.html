A route, also known as the Camino del Azahar, from Cartagena, where according to legend St James first landed, and Murcia, which joins the Camino del Sureste at Petrola near Albacete. See Cartagena Amigos website and CaminoPedia (this describes the route as joining the Camino del Sureste at Fuente-Alamo, but that now runs further north and no longer goes to Fuente-Alamo).
The first part to Murcia and Molina de Segura is coincident with the Caminos de la Cruz.
From Jumilla, there is an alternative route via Yecla to the Ruta de la Lana at Almansa. See Caminos de la Cruz for mapping for Jumilla-Yecla section.
