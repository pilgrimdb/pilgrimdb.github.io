Originally, the Kinzigtäler Jakobusweg was a route across the Schwarzwald from Lossburg to the Alsace route at Kehl, with a link from Rottenburg am Neckar created by the Jakobusgruppe Horb; a guide by Kurt Klein was published. This combined route was sometimes known as the Schwarzwälder Jakobsweg.
With the development of the Neckar-Baar-Jakobusweg, the route now leaves this at Horb; see Jakobuswege Schwarzwald Alb project.
