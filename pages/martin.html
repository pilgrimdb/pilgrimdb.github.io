The European Cultural Centre of St Martin of Tours is developing a series of routes linking places associated with the saint and centred on Tours.
Two local routes have been created, for which guides are available:
  • the 114km Chemin de l'Eté de la Saint-Martin from Chinon and Candes-Saint-Martin to Tours
  • and the 236km Chemin de l'Evêque de Tours from Poitiers and Ligugé to Tours
In addition, four international routes are scheduled for completion by the 1700th anniversary of the birth of St Martin in 2016:
  • the Via Sancti Martini from Szombathely in Hungary. See Via Sancti Martini for details of the Hungarian section together with other linking routes, and Via sancti Martini - France for details of the French section
  • from Saragossa in Aragon
  • from Trier in Germany; a guide is available for the last 138km of this, from Vendôme
  • from Utrecht

To simplify mapping, the mapping for the non-French sections of these routes are listed on a separate page.

See also Martinusweg.
