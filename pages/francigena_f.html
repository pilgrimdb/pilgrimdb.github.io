Despite the fact that a 'road from France' in France is a nonsensical concept, the whole of Sigeric's route, including the section in France, was adopted by the European Institute of Cultural Routes as one of their 'pilgrim pathways'. There is however no agreement on the exact line of any modern walking route, although a GR, the GR145, roughly based on the route, is gradually being assembled by local FFRP groups, currently scheduled for completion in 2013.
The Association Internationale Via Francigena published the first maps with a suggested route, but this is no longer available. Lightfoot have however published a guide in English with their suggested route, based on the AIVF's. In Pas de Calais, the GAL in Artois Lys has marked a route, for which a guide is available; however, this does not correspond with the AIVF or Lightfoot routes. The GR145 in Champagne-Ardenne has also been completed; and see Tourisme Langres for route in Hte-Marne.
There is also an Association Via Francigena France, which unites various organisations with an interest in developing routes.
