In 2003, the pilgrimage group Iubilantes, from Como, devised a route for a pilgrimage from Rome to Monte Sant'Angelo, and later published a book on their journey and route, Verso il Monte dell'Angelo. This route is now also known as the Via Micaelica. Their website includes a summary of the route. It does not follow the Roman roads, but instead takes a more direct route over the Appennines from Montecassino.
See also Rinaldo Cucchi's blog on his pilgrimage largely based on the Iubilantes route.
